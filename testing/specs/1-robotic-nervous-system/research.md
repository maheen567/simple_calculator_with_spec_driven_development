# Research for Module 1: The Robotic Nervous System (ROS 2)

## Autonomous Behaviors for Humanoid Robots in ROS 2

### Decision
Cover a blend of navigation, perception, and manipulation relevant to humanoid tasks.

### Rationale
Providing a comprehensive overview that touches upon navigation (path planning, obstacle avoidance), perception (sensor data processing, environment understanding), and manipulation (arm control, grasping) offers a more complete picture of how AI agents interact with humanoid robots using ROS 2. This multi-faceted approach caters to the intermediate AI student audience by demonstrating the breadth of ROS 2 capabilities in robotics.

### Alternatives Considered
-   **Navigation focus**: Concentrating solely on navigation might be too narrow and wouldn't showcase the full spectrum of AI-robot interaction.
-   **Perception focus**: While crucial, perception alone doesn't cover how AI makes decisions and controls the robot's actions.
-   **Manipulation focus**: Manipulation is key, but without understanding the robot's environment and movement capabilities, it's incomplete.

A blended approach provides a more holistic understanding.
